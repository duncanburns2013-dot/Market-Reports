STATE OF THE MARKET

A Quarter of Shifting Signals

The first quarter of 2026 brought a mix of cautious optimism and strategic recalibration across Greater Newburyport. After a winter that held on longer than most, spring inventory arrived with purpose — new listings rose modestly while buyers remained engaged but selective.

Pricing held firm in core markets, though year-over-year adjustments in several towns suggest the market is finding a more sustainable rhythm. Days on market ticked up slightly, giving buyers breathing room they haven't had in years.

For sellers, pricing right from day one remains the clearest path to a successful outcome. For buyers, expanding inventory means more choice, more leverage, and a window that may not stay open indefinitely.

LOCAL INSIGHT

Greater Newburyport:
Where Value Meets Character

Greater Newburyport remains one of the North Shore's most compelling markets — where historic New England charm meets coastal living and a community that punches well above its weight in culture, dining, and outdoor recreation.

This quarter, several towns experienced year-over-year price adjustments after an extraordinary run of appreciation. West Newbury and Newbury saw the most notable corrections, reflecting a normalization from peak pricing rather than any fundamental shift in demand. Inventory remains tight across the region at just 42 days on market, and well-priced homes in desirable locations continue to move with authority.

What the Gap Means

A "gap" of 2.28× means a Newburyport buyer needs 2.28 times the state median income to afford the average home — over $239K annually. Even Salisbury, the most affordable town in Greater Newburyport, requires 18% above median income.
